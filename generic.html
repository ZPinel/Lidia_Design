Set Design

After graduating from Gustavus Adolphus in 2011 with a BFA in studio art, Lidia completed her Master's of Fine Art from the University of Maryland in 2014. She has since worked for the Guthrie Theater as Assistant Set Desiner and Lead Construction Engineer while maintaining a full-time professorship at Gustavus Adolphus.

When asked about how she found herself in set design, Lidia answered "serendipity. I initially began grad school with a goal of finding a path in costume design or gallery management, but my work with miniatures my first semester flicked a proverbial lightbulb and I found myself in scenographic art." Finding her calling in the world of sceinc art was by no means and accident. Growing up hunting, fishing, and working on her family's large estate in rural Wisconsin helped form this artist's appreciation for the physicality of massive productions. Fine tuning her motor skills through miniature design married Lidia's skill for engineering and graphic design.

Lidia is now passing on her talents to future generations of set designers as a studio art professor at Gustavus Adolphus College, Lidia's alma mater. Smaller theaters are also fortunate to have access to Lidia's talents as she is a freelance consultant for set design across the Twin Cities metropolitan area.
